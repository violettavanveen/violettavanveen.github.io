About

December 21, 2021   

I am a master’s student in Public Policy and Development at Paris School of Economics. I graduated summa cum laude in July 2021 with a bachelor’s in Economics, Markets and Institutions at the University of Bologna, where I was a fellow at Collegio Superiore.

My research interests lie primarily in the field of labor economics, in general as well as in particular from a gender perspective. This is a field I have been interested in since taking a course in Labor Economics during my bachelor’s as additional coursework beyond the requirements for my degree. The interest for this field led me to choose to work on the gender division of labor in households for my Bachelor’s final project (see projects section). Currently, I am also working with other students from different Italian honors colleges (Collegio Superiore, Scuola Normale Superiore, Scuola Galileiana di Studi Superiori) on a paper concerning an empirical analysis of the factors that influence admission to such schools.

I have previous experience in research, having worked as a research intern and collaborated on a paper which is currently under review. In addition, I have extensive experience in technical and scientific writing thanks to the coursework required for my bachelor’s fellowship.

During my bachelor’s, I was the TA for the seminar in Equity and Efficiency in Taxation Policies at Collegio Superiore. Furthermore, I have been on exchange at Tilburg University, with a study track focused on econometrics and public administration.

I am proficient in advanced use of the R programming language for applications in econometrics and have used the RMarkdown framework in projects in labor economics and analyses of inequality, as well as for a comprehensive replication study during my exchange.

You can reach me on LinkedIn or via e-mail at violettavanveen at gmail.com.
